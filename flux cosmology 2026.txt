Quantum–Entropic Flux Cosmology: A Unified Mechanism
Without Dark Energy or Inflation
Jamie McCabe
March 14, 2026
Abstract
We present a reduced effective cosmological model driven by a single entropy-deficit scalar Φ,
whose dynamics generate accelerated expansion and modified structure growth without introducing a cosmological-constant dark-energy sector or a separate inflaton field. Cosmic expansion
˙ emerges from an entropy-production law, S= γΦ, coupled to a flux contribution in the Friedmann equation, while early-universe causal connectivity is enhanced through a deficit-dependent
effective propagation speed ceff(Φ, QΦ, Π) that reaches factors of order 6–8 near recombination
in the viable region of the model. Quantum steering is supplied by a Bohm-style flux-adapted
potential QΦ, and classicalization is modeled through a measurement-induced projection (MIP)
density Π.
The framework is formulated as an effective field theory with non-minimal curvature coupling
via Geff(Φ, Π), together with flux-trace coupling and MIP back-reaction terms. In the homogeneous limit, this yields a master system which, after smoothing with a square-root closure
and mild damping, supports a compact continuous viable region in parameter space centered
near α ≈ 8, χQ ≈ 0.05–0.06, and χΠ ≈ 0.03–0.04. Within this basin, the model produces: (i)
sufficient early causal enlargement to address the horizon problem, (ii) late-time acceleration
with w(0) ≈ −0.87 to −0.92, and (iii) a linear growth index γ(z = 1) ≈ 0.499–0.505, while
keeping max(Geff/G) < 1.09 and local lunar signatures below 2 × 10−11
.
The theory recovers standard general relativity in the low-deficit, high-projection limit and
remains diffeomorphism-invariant at the effective level. Although the present treatment is a
homogeneous proof-of-mechanism and a full perturbative derivation remains future work, it
demonstrates that a unified entropy-quantum-projection framework can reproduce horizon, acceleration, and reduced-growth phenomenology within a nontrivial parameter island.

1 Introduction
Modern cosmology rests on a remarkably successful but patchwork framework. The standard
ΛCDM model explains a broad range of observations, from the cosmic microwave background
acoustic peaks to large-scale structure and type Ia supernova distances, yet it relies on largely distinct sectors to address separate problems: an inflaton-like mechanism for early causal smoothing, a
dark-energy component for late-time acceleration, and dark matter for enhanced clustering. While
phenomenologically effective, this layered structure leaves open the question of whether a more
unified physical mechanism could underlie all three [3].
This paper explores such a candidate within a reduced effective framework we term Flux Cosmology. The central dynamical quantity is an entropy-deficit scalar

Conceptually, the framework borrows elements from scalar–tensor gravity [1], variable-speedof-light proposals, and entropic gravity approaches, but combines them into a single entropy-deficit
driven mechanism. In the homogeneous FLRW limit, the model reduces to a compact master
system that can be evolved numerically and tested against broad phenomenological targets: horizon
enlargement, late-time acceleration, and a reduced linear growth index.
We emphasize that the present treatment is a homogeneous proof-of-mechanism implementation
using proxy closures for the Laplacian structure in QΦ and the MIP operator. Full perturbation
theory, a more principled derivation of the projection operator, and detailed confrontation with
precision CMB, BAO, and lensing datasets remain future work. Nevertheless, the reduced system already demonstrates that a unified entropy-quantum-projection framework can generate a
continuous viable parameter basin rather than a single isolated tuned point.

56 Discussion and Outlook
The present construction is best interpreted as a proof-of-mechanism effective model rather than
a final fundamental theory. Several important limitations remain. First, the Bohm-style potential
and MIP contribution are implemented through homogeneous proxy closures rather than through
a full covariant perturbative derivation. Second, the growth analysis is limited to linear structure
formation within an effective Geff description and has not yet been confronted with full CMB, BAO,
and weak-lensing likelihoods. Third, the operator-level definition of the MIP projector remains to
be developed.
Despite these limitations, the model establishes a coherent and numerically tractable framework in which entropy deficit, quantum steering, projection-induced classicalization, and effective
propagation speed are linked within a single cosmological mechanism. The existence of a compact
continuous viable basin, rather than a single fine-tuned point, is the principal result of the present
paper.
Future work should extend the perturbative sector to derive scalar and tensor spectra explicitly,
refine the projector algebra to move δLbranch beyond placeholder status, test the model against
precision datasets, and perform a self-consistent two-pass calibration of kflux using the evolved
S(ttoday) rather than a fixed guess.
