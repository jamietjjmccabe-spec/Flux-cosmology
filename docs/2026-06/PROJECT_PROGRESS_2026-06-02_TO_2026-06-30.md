# Flux/MIP project progress: 2-30 June 2026

This document records the principal theoretical, numerical, and terminology work completed during the 28-day interval ending 30 June 2026. It is a development record, not a claim that the model has been empirically established.

## 1. Terminology and ontology

- **MIP is now fixed as Minimum Interface Point.** The older expansion “Measurement-Induced Projection” is retired. A MIP is treated as a smallest permitted interface at which quantum-state information can update or exchange.
- The theory now distinguishes **causal ordering** from **experienced duration**. Time is represented as an ordered point-to-point update structure; duration is an emergent local measure related to the number and density of environmental interactions between ordered updates.
- **BMH** is treated as accumulated interaction or registration history. In the active interpretation, curvature is not simply a count of events but the covariant macroscopic response associated with the retained history field.
- Wave/particle language was reframed as a registration-depth distinction: weakly registered propagation remains wave-dominant, while strongly environmentally registered states behave classically. This remains a conceptual hypothesis pending a microscopic derivation.

## 2. Micro-to-macro bridge

A three-stage bridge was formalized:

1. Planck-scale Minimum Interface Point updates.
2. A coarse-grained update-density or registration-depth field.
3. A bounded macroscopic response gate.

The working registration variable is

\[
D_{\rm reg}(x,t)=\int_{t_{\rm form}}^t \bar n_{\rm MIP}(x,t')W(t,t')\,dt',
\]

with a retention kernel \(W\). The bounded activation gate is

\[
G_{\rm bound}=\frac{D_{\rm reg}^q}{D_{\rm reg}^q+D_*^q}.
\]

The exponent \(q\) controls whether the response is gradual or threshold-like. This avoids direct summation of Planck-scale nodes and makes the proposal numerically tractable at galaxy scale.

## 3. Dark-matter sector: metric memory formulation

The galaxy-sector observable was sharpened from a generic “missing mass” statement to a gravitational differential:

\[
\Delta g_{\rm Flux}=g_{\rm observed}-g_{\rm baryon}.
\]

The active phenomenological interpretation is that this differential is generated by a registration potential \(\Phi_{\rm reg}\):

\[
\Delta g_{\rm Flux}=\nabla \Phi_{\rm reg},
\qquad
\rho_{\rm DM,eff}=\frac{1}{4\pi G}\nabla^2\Phi_{\rm reg}.
\]

A bounded multiplicative form was identified for numerical fitting:

\[
g_{\rm total}=g_N\left[1+A_D G_{\rm bound}G_{\rm ret}G_{\rm env}\right].
\]

This is a falsifiable effective description. It does not yet constitute a covariant derivation of the source tensor.

## 4. Covariant registration sector and conservation

The perturbative registration framework was advanced with the following constraints:

- Registration feedback may depend locally on baryon density and baryon expansion/convergence.
- A representative stable feedback point was identified at approximately \(b_\rho=1\), \(b_\Theta=-0.5\), meaning overdensity and convergence enhance registration.
- The energy exchanged during registration is balanced by an explicit binding/build reservoir, preserving the background and perturbed Bianchi identities.
- The perturbation variables retained for the frozen pre-integration baseline are \(\{\delta V,\delta\rho_{\rm reg},q_{\rm reg}\}\), together with the diagnostics \(\{\epsilon_Q,\epsilon_{\rm birth},R_{\rm force}\}\).

## 5. Perturbation-stability protocol

The stability scan was strengthened from a simple logarithmic wavenumber sweep to a nested complex-frequency protocol:

- use physical wavenumber \(K=k/a\), not bare comoving \(k\);
- scan a broad backbone, approximately \(K/H\in[10^{-2},10^6]\);
- refine adaptively near relaxation, effective-mass, and resonance scales;
- track eigenvalue branches using both eigenvalue distance and eigenvector overlap;
- reject ghost, gradient, tachyonic, and narrow resonance instabilities;
- retain a strict no-artificial-floor policy.

This protocol is intended to prevent false stability caused by missed narrow instability bands or branch swapping at avoided crossings.

## 6. Scalar-mediated force path

The local dark-matter replacement path was narrowed to a scalar-mediated fifth-force formulation in which the scalar is a mediator rather than an additional local mass density:

\[
\mathbf a_\phi=-q_b\nabla\phi.
\]

This avoids one form of local energy-density double counting but creates a stringent three-way constraint:

1. a galactic-scale force large enough to affect rotation curves;
2. suppression or screening in the Solar System and laboratory environments;
3. freedom from scalar ghosts, gradient instabilities, or unacceptable radiative behavior.

The path remains open but unvalidated.

## 7. Antimatter and charge-conjugation bookkeeping

The theory was constrained to respect the observed downward gravitational acceleration of antihydrogen. The gravitational source is therefore charge-conjugation even. A bookkeeping split was adopted:

- \(D_+\): total localized energy-history ledger, including matter and antimatter, and therefore gravitational;
- \(D_-\): charge or matter-antimatter asymmetry ledger, treated as a current-like quantity rather than a negative gravitational source.

Antimatter is not used as negative mass or antigravity.

## 9. Black-hole and temporal-depth interpretation

Black-hole work during the interval emphasized:

- BMH as accumulated interaction history contributing to the macroscopic curvature description;
- the need for a non-singular interior or effective cutoff rather than treating a coordinate singularity as a physical endpoint;

The existing black-hole scripts remain toy models and should not be read as numerical relativity.

## 10. Cosmology integration boundary

The pre-integration model remains frozen pending a full unmodified CLASS implementation with

\[
\Omega_{\rm cdm}=0.
\]

No claim of CMB or growth success is accepted from background-only integrals or CPL substitution. The required next stage is a genuine Boltzmann-code integration of the exact perturbation equations, followed by CMB, lensing, matter-power, RSD, and stability diagnostics.

## 11. Thin-disk numerical lesson

A razor-thin self-gravitating sheet was found to be resolution-pathological: without a physical vertical thickness or ultraviolet cutoff, fragmentation cascades to the grid scale. The project therefore rejects non-converged clump statistics from this baseline. Future disk tests must include a physical thickness, pressure/dispersion scale, or other justified small-scale regulator.

A new exoplanet screening layer was implemented:

- **ECEE**: Earth Coherence Envelope Equivalence, a functional-equivalence score;
- **Planetary battery**: a heuristic internal-maintenance score using rocky-boundary, internal heat, retention, dynamo, tidal, and stellar-stress proxies.

The June run produced:

- a reusable scoring script;
- a 6,224-row scored NASA-archive working table;
- a 57-object usable-candidate table;

These scores are hypothesis-generating only. Inferred masses, radii, atmospheres, magnetic fields, stellar ages, and activity proxies can dominate the ranking. Candidate labels must not be interpreted as evidence of habitability.

## 13. Current status

The project has a clearer vocabulary, a bounded registration field, a falsifiable galaxy residual, a conservation-aware perturbation sector, and a more rigorous stability protocol. Its main scientific bottlenecks remain:

- deriving the registration stress tensor from an action;
- demonstrating a healthy scalar/registration parameter region;
- passing local fifth-force and equivalence-principle constraints;
- completing CLASS integration at \(\Omega_{\rm cdm}=0\);
- fitting galaxy data without system-by-system tuning;
- separating predictive observables from interpretive language.

The theory is therefore best described as an organized, falsifiable research program with several working phenomenological modules, not as a confirmed replacement for general relativity or \(\Lambda\)CDM.
