# Dark matter as a metric-memory differential

## Working interpretation

Flux/MIP does not assume that the galaxy residual must be an additional collisionless particle density. Instead it tests whether the residual can be modeled as a persistent field generated by baryonic history.

The concise hypothesis is:

> The effective dark component is the gravitational response associated with retained baryonic registration history.

This is a model hypothesis, not an observational conclusion.

## Fundamental residual

The primary observable is

\[
\Delta g_{\rm Flux}(r,t)
=
g_{\rm observed}(r,t)-g_{\rm baryon}(r,t).
\]

Introduce a registration potential \(\Phi_{\rm reg}\) such that

\[
\Delta g_{\rm Flux}=\nabla\Phi_{\rm reg}.
\]

An observer analyzing the same field with the standard Poisson equation would infer

\[
\rho_{\rm DM,eff}
=
\frac{1}{4\pi G}\nabla^2\Phi_{\rm reg}.
\]

The inferred density is therefore an effective representation of the registration field; it is not automatically a material density.

## History functional

A generic nonlocal form is

\[
D_{\rm reg}(x,t)
=
\int_{t_{\rm form}}^t
\rho_b(x,t')\,\mathcal K(x,t')\,\mathcal R(x,t')\,W(t,t')\,dt',
\]

followed by

\[
\Phi_{\rm reg}=\mathcal F[D_{\rm reg}].
\]

Here \(\mathcal K\) is a collapse/compression weight, \(\mathcal R\) a retention factor, and \(W\) a memory kernel.

A practical bounded fitting law is

\[
g_{\rm total}
=
g_N\left[1+A_DG_{\rm bound}G_{\rm ret}G_{\rm env}\right],
\]

or

\[
\frac{g_{\rm observed}-g_N}{g_N}
=
A_DG_{\rm bound}G_{\rm ret}G_{\rm env}.
\]

## Required tests

A viable history model must predict, with shared parameters:

- galaxy rotation curves and their scatter;
- the radial-acceleration relation;
- weak and strong lensing;
- dwarf-galaxy dynamics;
- cluster dynamics, including merging systems;
- structure growth and the matter power spectrum;
- CMB lensing and primary anisotropies;
- negligible anomalous effects in the Solar System and laboratories.

It must also specify whether \(\Phi_{\rm reg}\) is local, retarded, or genuinely nonlocal, and show that the resulting equations are causal and conserve stress energy.

## Distinction from dark energy

The local residual is associated with gradients of registration depth. The cosmological acceleration sector is instead represented by a global flux/coherence contribution. Symbolically,

\[
\Delta H^2_{\rm Flux}
=
H^2_{\rm observed}-H^2_{\rm matter+radiation}.
\]

The local and global sectors must not be conflated or counted twice in the stress tensor.

## Scientific status

“Metric memory” is currently a phenomenological organizing principle. It becomes a physical theory only after an action, field equations, conservation law, screening mechanism, and data fit are supplied.
